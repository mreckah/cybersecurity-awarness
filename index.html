Introduction

Cybersecurity is a shared responsibility across the entire organization. Every employee, regardless of their role, interacts with systems, data, and networks that can be targeted by attackers.

Simple daily actions — such as opening an email, clicking a link, connecting to Wi-Fi, or installing software — can create vulnerabilities if not handled carefully.

Cybercriminals often exploit human behavior rather than technical weaknesses. Your awareness and vigilance are the best defenses.

Social Engineering

Attackers manipulate people into revealing confidential information or taking unsafe actions — no hacking required.

Common Attack Types

Phishing

Fake emails that look real, designed to steal your login or data. Always check the sender's actual email address.

Vishing

Attackers call pretending to be IT support or your bank. Never share passwords over the phone.

Smishing

Phishing via SMS text messages. Treat all unexpected links in texts with suspicion.

Oversharing Online

Posting work details on social media gives attackers free intelligence. Keep professional information private.

Risks

  • Identity theft
  • Unauthorized access
  • Financial loss
  • Data breach

Best Practices

  • Verify the sender before responding
  • Do not click on suspicious links
  • Never share passwords or sensitive data
  • Be suspicious of urgent or unusual requests

Wireless Security

Wireless networks, especially public ones, are often unsecured and can be exploited to intercept your data.

Never access sensitive company data or banking apps on public Wi-Fi without a VPN. Attackers may create fake hotspots with familiar names.

Home Wi-Fi Safety

  • Change the default router password
  • Use WPA2 or WPA3 encryption
  • Keep router firmware up to date
  • Disable auto-connect to unknown networks

Using a VPN

  • Use the corporate VPN on all public networks
  • Connect before accessing company resources
  • Keep VPN software up to date

Account Security

User accounts are one of the most targeted entry points. A compromised account can give an attacker access to everything you can access.

Strong Passwords

Use passphrases like "BlueHorse!RunsFast". Avoid short or obvious passwords. Never reuse passwords across different sites.

  • Minimum 12 characters
  • Mix letters, numbers, and symbols
  • Use a password manager

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Even if a password is stolen, MFA prevents attackers from logging in. It requires a second verification — like a code sent to your phone.

Enable MFA on all work accounts, email, and cloud apps.
Data breaches expose millions of passwords. Check haveibeenpwned.com to see if your email has been leaked, and change affected passwords immediately.

Software & Internet Security

Software vulnerabilities are one of the most common ways attackers gain access. Keeping software updated is one of the simplest and most effective defenses.

Updates & Patching

Updates fix known security holes (CVEs). Delaying them leaves the door open for attackers. Enable automatic updates whenever possible.

Malware & Ransomware

Malware can steal, delete, or encrypt your files. Ransomware locks you out until you pay. Never download attachments or software from unknown sources.

How a Cyber Attack Works

Hybrid Work Security

Working remotely increases exposure. Treat your device and its data as if they are the company's most valuable asset.

Physical Security

Lock your screen when stepping away (Win+L / Cmd+Ctrl+Q). Never leave a device unattended in public, even for a moment.

Device Protection

Enable full-disk encryption. Keep antivirus active. Report any lost or stolen device to IT immediately — every minute counts.

Mobile Threats

Only install apps from official app stores. Be alert for smishing (fraudulent SMS) targeting your work phone number.

Scenario: Stolen Laptop

An employee leaves their laptop in a café for 5 minutes. It is stolen. If the disk is not encrypted and the screen is unlocked, an attacker has immediate access to all company systems and files. Encryption + screen lock = time to respond.

Data Security

Data is one of the most valuable and most targeted assets of any organization. Protecting it is both a legal obligation and an ethical responsibility.

Intellectual Property

Source code, product designs, trade secrets. Never share externally without explicit authorization.

Customer Data

Personal and financial information protected by GDPR and other laws. A breach can result in major fines and permanent loss of trust.

Employee Data

HR records, health info, salaries. Treat this with the highest level of confidentiality.

Risks

  • Data leaks
  • Unauthorized sharing
  • Compliance violations (GDPR)

Best Practices

  • Share only when necessary
  • Follow company policies
  • Store data in approved systems only
